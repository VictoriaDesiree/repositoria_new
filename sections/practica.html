“The Big Three”

trust:

to have confidence in something, to know that someone is telling the truth
Example sentence: It's important for people to trust their bank, otherwise they would not put their money there.

hold on:

an instruction to wait or pause.
Example sentence:The receptionist on the phone asked me to hold on for a few minutes while she connected me to Mr. Jenkins.

brute:

strong and not very refined
Example sentence: The man lifted the giant rock using his brute strength; he did not use any weightlifting technique.

sorcerer:

a person who claims to use magic.
Example sentence: In the film, a sorcerer transformed the prince into a frog through the use of magic.

Rationalize

to try to explain or justify (something) logically
Example sentence: The manager tried to rationalize her decision to fire Jen by telling herself that she didn't really enjoy working there anyway.

choice:

a decision; a selection from among possible options.
Example sentence:You have the choice of using paper bags or plastic bags when you buy things at the grocery store.

save:

to rescue; to preserve; to keep safe.
Example sentence: Firefighters work every day to save people, animals, and property from fires.

command:

the control of a group of people, especially in the military.
Example sentence: The ship is under the command of Captain Anderson. He has full control of all its sailors and operations.

Football in the Office

corporate:

short for "corporate office" or "head office", the headquarters of a company were senior management is located
Example sentence: If you have an issue with your proposed new work contract, you should contact >Human Resources at corporate to talk about it.

cooped up:

kept inside a building or in a small space, especially for a long period of time.
Example sentence:Kids, go out and play! You shouldn't stay cooped up inside all day when the weather is so nice outside.

think this through:

to consider all the different aspects of something in order to better understand it or make a decision.
Example sentence: If we don't think this through before we begin building the house, we could really waste a lot of time, money and effort.

either:

one or the other of two possible options.
Example sentence: You can either rent a car for the day, or take the train.
